Pupil Dilation: Controls iris aperture diameter. At rest (~3mm), the pupil admits moderate light. Fully dilated (~8mm, mydriasis) — as occurs in darkness or with clinical drops like tropicamide — maximizes light entry. Constriction (miosis) protects the retina from bright light and increases depth of focus.

Lens Accommodation: Simulates ciliary muscle action on the crystalline lens. When relaxed (slider at 0), zonular ligaments are taut, pulling the lens flat — focused at infinity. When accommodated (slider at 1), the ciliary muscle contracts, zonules slacken, and the elastic lens rounds — shifting focus to near objects. This ability weakens with age (presbyopia). Reset returns to the resting flat state for distance vision.

Glaucoma: Elevated intraocular pressure (IOP) from impaired aqueous humor drainage through the trabecular meshwork. The increased pressure damages retinal ganglion cell axons at the optic nerve head, causing progressive “cupping” of the optic disc. Vision loss begins peripherally and advances toward central vision if untreated. Animation phases: aqueous buildup → optic disc cupping → nerve fiber damage → peripheral field loss.

Retinal Detachment: The neural retina separates from the retinal pigment epithelium (RPE). Vitreous traction pulls on the retina creating a tear (most common in the superior-temporal quadrant). Fluid seeps through the tear, lifting the retina away. Patients experience floaters, photopsia (light flashes), and a descending shadow or “curtain” across their visual field. Animation phases: vitreous traction → tear formation → progressive peeling → curtain descent.
Enable Ultrasonic Depth Scan
Diagnostic Scanner simulates clinical imaging: B-Scan ultrasound and OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography). The false-color depth map reveals internal structural layers — corneal thickness, anterior chamber depth, lens position, and retinal layer integrity. Clinicians use these scans to detect macular edema, retinal thinning, glaucoma-related nerve fiber layer loss, and anterior chamber angle abnormalities. The cross-section clipping plane exposes the eye's internal architecture. The grid overlay simulates scan-line artifacts of real ultrasonic imaging equipment.
